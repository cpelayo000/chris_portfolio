For as long as I could remember I've had a deep love for fictional books. I was always pretty good at visualizing what was going on in the text, and would more often than not become attached to the main protagonist/antagonist. The only actual flaw I ever found in books was the inability to influence a character's fate, hence my interest in video games. All of a sudden I could read (and see) a great story while also having control of this main character. It didn't take long for me to fall in love with the idea of video games although another flaw had occurred. I could no longer picture the story in my own way since it was already a fixed visualization of someone else. However, in the 9th grade I discovered a solution to this problem. I could create my own game.

I began programming in August of 2016 and have loved every moment of it. I started out at the Los Angeles Film School under the Game Production Program but felt that the curriculum was a little slow. I did however take a class there on C# with Unity fundamentals, In which I sort of fell in love with programming. After a few months I dropped out and attended the Los Angeles Coding Dojo to further the little knowledge I had in the matter. During my stay at the coding bootcamp I became a proficient full-stack developer in Ruby on Rails and MEAN.
